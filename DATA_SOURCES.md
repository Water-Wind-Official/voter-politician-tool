# Data Sources for Representatives

This document lists reliable public sources for finding current representative information.

## Official Government Sources

### Federal Representatives (House & Senate)

1. **Congress.gov** - https://www.congress.gov/members
   - Official directory of all members of Congress
   - Includes contact information, committee assignments, and voting records
   - Most authoritative source

2. **House.gov** - https://www.house.gov/representatives
   - Official House of Representatives directory
   - Find representatives by state/district
   - Official contact information

3. **Senate.gov** - https://www.senate.gov/senators
   - Official Senate directory
   - Contact information and office locations
   - Committee assignments

4. **Congressional Directory** - https://www.congress.gov/members/find-your-member
   - Find your representative tool
   - Official contact forms

## Third-Party Public Sources

1. **Open States** - https://openstates.org
   - Comprehensive API and data for state and federal representatives
   - Well-maintained and regularly updated
   - Free API access available

2. **Ballotpedia** - https://ballotpedia.org
   - Non-partisan political encyclopedia
   - Includes biographies, voting records, and contact information
   - Good for historical context

3. **Vote Smart** - https://votesmart.org
   - Non-profit voter education organization
   - Comprehensive candidate and official information
   - Voting records and positions

4. **GovTrack** - https://www.govtrack.us
   - Tracks Congress and voting records
   - API available for developers
   - Good for legislative history

## What Information is Public Record

All of the following are considered public record and safe to include:

✅ **Name** - Official name as listed in public records
✅ **State & District** - Public electoral information
✅ **Party Affiliation** - Public political information
✅ **Office Address** - Congressional office address (public)
✅ **Office Phone** - Congressional office phone (public)
✅ **Official Email** - Congressional office email (public)
✅ **Official Website** - Publicly available website
✅ **Official Social Media** - Public social media accounts
✅ **Biography** - From official sources or public records
✅ **Term Dates** - Public electoral information
✅ **Voting Records** - Public legislative records

## What NOT to Include

❌ Personal home addresses
❌ Personal phone numbers
❌ Personal email addresses
❌ Private social media accounts
❌ Family information
❌ Financial information (unless from required disclosures)
❌ Any non-public information

## Legal Compliance

- All information must be from publicly available sources
- Information about elected officials is generally considered public record
- Congressional office contact information is specifically public record
- When in doubt, only use information from official government websites
- Do not scrape or collect data in violation of website terms of service

## Data Collection Best Practices

1. **Verify Sources** - Always verify information from official sources
2. **Regular Updates** - Representative information changes after elections
3. **Accuracy** - Double-check all contact information
4. **Attribution** - Note where information came from when possible
5. **Respect Privacy** - Only use public, official information

## API Resources

If you want to automate data collection:

- **Congress.gov API** - Requires API key, official source
- **Open States API** - Free tier available, comprehensive
- **ProPublica Congress API** - Discontinued but historical data available
- **GovTrack API** - Free, good for voting records
